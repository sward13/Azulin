Human-Centered AI Adoption

A Proven Framework for AI Transformation That Actually Sticks

Most AI transformations fail because organizations treat them as technology projects when they're actually behavior change challenges. Research from Boston Consulting Group found that only 26% of companies see tangible ROI from AI investments. The culprit isn't the technology—it's human behavior.

People resist change. They stick with familiar methods even when AI offers better outcomes (status quo bias). They overreact to AI mistakes while forgiving identical errors from humans (algorithm aversion). They won't experiment if failure feels career-limiting (lack of psychological safety). These behavioral barriers—not technical limitations—are what kill adoption.

Human-centered AI adoption addresses these barriers systematically. After years leading enterprise AI transformation—moving thousands of people through change in risk-averse environments—I've learned that success comes from understanding behavioral resistance, designing specific interventions to overcome it, and measuring actual adoption rather than just training completion.

The implementation scars are real, but so are the results: 6-10x adoption multipliers, measurable knowledge gains, and change that sticks beyond the initial rollout.
